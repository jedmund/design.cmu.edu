Overview

This page is documentation for the interaction prototype for a new School of Design website, available at this site.

The intent of this page is to highlight insights that we have learned over the course of a semester researching the school and its key constituencies, as well as document the various interaction and visual standards that we have established and the thought processes behind their inception.

At the bottom of the page is a diagram of which pages are presently functional in this prototype. Thusly, that diagram may, and probably will, change over time.

Takeaways

  • Importance of Information

    The website is the main source of information about the school and should be treated as a priority.

    Information should always manifest in the format that it wants to be in. Each piece of information should be treated as its own designed deliverable.

    The web is capable of good typography and hierarchy and should reflect the aesthetic values of the school.

  • Process Culture

    The importance of process and documentation should be indoctrinated in the students. They present narratives of thought, not necessarily final products.

    We aren't creating a gallery because we present stories, not slideshows.

    The new gallery structure isn't creating more work, it's a natural step forward from physical process books. It's more environmental and accessible for everyone.

  • Involving the Community

    All parties we've spoken to agree that there is not enough transparency and overlap between different groups within the school.

    There's also not enough transparency with alumni interested in our progress. Everyone should be a part of one conversation.

    Given an outlet, students will create high-quality content that drives internal, and consequently external, culture.
